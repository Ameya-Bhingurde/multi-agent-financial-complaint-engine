CREDIT CARD POLICY KNOWLEDGE BASE
Document: Capital One Credit Card Agreement — Key Provisions
Source: Capital One Bank (USA), N.A. — Standard Consumer Credit Card Agreement (2024)
Applicable Cards: Capital One Venture, Quicksilver, Savor, Platinum, Secured Mastercard

========================================================================
SECTION 1: BILLING RIGHTS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
========================================================================

1.1 IN CASE OF ERRORS OR QUESTIONS ABOUT YOUR BILL

If you think there is an error on your bill, write to us at:
  Capital One, P.O. Box 30285, Salt Lake City, UT 84130-0285
  Or call: 1-800-CAPITAL (1-800-227-4825)

In your letter, provide:
  - Your name and account number.
  - The dollar amount of the suspected error.
  - A description of the error and why you believe it is an error.

You must contact us within 60 DAYS of the date the first bill containing the error was sent.

1.2 WHAT WE WILL DO

We must acknowledge your letter within 30 days UNLESS we resolve the dispute first.

Within two billing cycles (no more than 90 days) we will either:
  (a) Correct the error; OR
  (b) Explain why we believe the bill was correct.

While we investigate:
  - We cannot try to collect the disputed amount.
  - We cannot report you as delinquent for the disputed amount.
  - We cannot close or restrict your account solely because of the dispute.
  - We may continue to charge interest on the disputed amount.
  - We may apply the disputed amount to your credit limit.

1.3 YOUR RIGHTS IF YOU ARE DISSATISFIED WITH CREDIT CARD PURCHASES

If you are dissatisfied with goods or services that you have purchased with your credit card and have tried in good faith to correct the problem with the merchant, you may have the right not to pay the remaining amount due on the purchase.

To use this right, all of the following must be true:
  (a) The purchase must have been made using your credit card in your home state or within 100 miles of your current mailing address. (This limitation does not apply if Capital One owns the business you purchased from.)
  (b) The purchase price must have been more than $50.
  (c) You must have made a good faith effort to resolve the dispute with the merchant first.
  (d) You must not have paid for the purchase.

SECTION 2: UNAUTHORIZED CHARGES AND FRAUD PROTECTION
========================================================================

2.1 ZERO LIABILITY POLICY

Capital One's $0 Fraud Liability Guarantee:
  - Cardholders are NOT responsible for unauthorized charges.
  - This applies to ALL unauthorized credit card transactions.
  - Must report unauthorized charges promptly.

2.2 FEDERAL LAW PROTECTIONS

Under federal law (Fair Credit Billing Act):
  - Maximum liability for unauthorized use: $50.
  - Must notify Capital One on back of card within a reasonable time.
  - Notification reduces further liability to $0.

2.3 HOW TO REPORT FRAUD

  - App: Select transaction → "Report a Problem."
  - Website: Sign in → Account History → Select transaction → Report fraud.
  - Phone: Call number on back of card.
  - Charges older than 6 months: Must call.

Investigation timelines:
  - Standard fraud investigation: Up to 90 days.
  - Provisional credit may be issued during investigation.
  - Replacement card: Typically arrives within 4–6 business days.

2.4 CONSUMER RESPONSIBILITY LIMITATIONS

  Cardholders ARE responsible for:
  - Transactions made by authorized users (people cardholder gave the card to).
  - Transactions above credit limit (Consumer responsible for full amount).
  - Interest charges on authorized transactions.
  - Fees assessed in accordance with the Agreement.

  Cardholders should take reasonable steps to prevent unauthorized use:
  - Keep card number confidential.
  - Report loss or theft immediately.
  - Do not share card details with unknown parties.

SECTION 3: RATES AND FEES
========================================================================

3.1 ANNUAL PERCENTAGE RATES (Variable — tied to Prime Rate)

  Purchase APR: Prime Rate + 7.74% to Prime Rate + 21.74% (based on creditworthiness)
  Balance Transfer APR: Same as Purchase APR or promotional rate.
  Cash Advance APR: Prime Rate + 21.74% (typically highest).
  Penalty APR: 29.99% fixed or variable.
    - Applies: After 2 consecutive late payments.
    - Review: Capital One will review after 6 months of on-time minimum payments.

Prime Rate: Highest rate published in The Wall Street Journal on the last business day of each month.

3.2 FEES

  Annual Fee:
    - Varies by card. $0 for Platinum and Quicksilver. Up to $95 for Venture. $95 for Savor.

  Late Payment Fee:
    - Up to $40 (typically $32 for first late payment, $40 for subsequent).

  Cash Advance Fee:
    - 3% to 5% of each advance, minimum $3 to $10.

  Balance Transfer Fee:
    - 3% to 5% of each transfer amount (some promotional offers: $0).

  Foreign Transaction Fee:
    - $0 (Capital One does NOT charge foreign transaction fees on any card).

  Returned Payment Fee: Up to $40.

  Over-Limit Fee: $0 — Capital One does not charge over-limit fees. Transactions that would exceed limit are typically declined.

3.3 GRACE PERIOD

  - 25 days minimum grace period (time from statement close to payment due date).
  - If you pay in full by the due date, no interest on purchases.
  - No grace period for cash advances (interest from date of transaction).
  - If you carry a balance, interest accrues on new purchases from transaction date.

3.4 MINIMUM PAYMENT

  - Each month: greater of $25 OR 2% of the statement balance.
  - If balance is less than $25: pay the full balance.
  - Paying only minimum: Extends payoff time significantly and increases total interest paid.

SECTION 4: ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT
========================================================================

4.1 CREDIT LIMIT

  - Capital One sets initial credit limit based on creditworthiness.
  - Capital One may increase, decrease, restrict, or cancel credit limits at any time.
  - Limit changes do not affect obligation to pay existing balance.
  - Automatic credit limit increases may be offered; consumer may opt out.

4.2 RATE AND TERM CHANGES

  - Capital One may change rates, fees, and terms.
  - 45 days advance notice required for adverse changes.
  - Consumer may opt out: Must notify Capital One of desire to close account within 45-day notice period. Existing balance paid off at prior terms.
  - Changes applicable to existing balances: PROHIBITED except after 60 days delinquency (CARD Act protection).

4.3 ACCOUNT CLOSURE

  Reasons Capital One may close or suspend account:
  - Default on this or any other Capital One account.
  - Failure to comply with Agreement terms.
  - Use of account for fraudulent or unlawful purposes.
  - Bankruptcy filing.
  - Account inactivity for an extended period.

  Capital One WILL NOT close account solely because consumer filed a billing dispute.

========================================================================
SECTION 5: CREDIT CARD ACCOUNT SECURITY TOOLS
========================================================================

5.1 CARD LOCK

  - Consumer can lock card instantly via app or website.
  - Prevents new purchases, cash advances, and balance transfers.
  - Does NOT prevent: recurring charges, returns, or credits.
  - Unlock instantly when card is found.

5.2 VIRTUAL CARD NUMBERS (ENO)

  - Capital One's Eno feature generates virtual card numbers for online shopping.
  - Virtual number links to real account but is unique to each merchant.
  - If compromised, replace virtual number without changing main card.

5.3 ALERTS

  - Consumer can set up real-time alerts for:
      - Transactions above specified amount.
      - Transactions in foreign countries.
      - Recurring charges.
      - Account balance milestones.
  - 2-way text alerts for suspicious activity (consumer can immediately confirm or deny).

5.4 CREDIT MONITORING (CreditWise)

  - Free credit monitoring available to all consumers (even non-Capital One customers).
  - Alerts for changes to credit report.
  - Does not affect credit score.

========================================================================
SECTION 6: REWARDS AND MILES PROGRAM TERMS
========================================================================

6.1 MILES EARNING

  - Venture: 2X miles per dollar on every purchase.
  - Quicksilver: 1.5% cash back on all purchases.
  - Savor: 4% on dining and entertainment, 3% on grocery stores.
  - Miles/cash back earned on net purchases (not returns, credits, or fees).

6.2 REDEEMING REWARDS

  - Miles: Redeem for travel at 1 cent per mile, or transfer to partner airlines/hotels.
  - Cash back: Redeem for statement credit, check, or gift cards.
  - Minimum redemption: Varies by card (typically $25 for cash back).

6.3 REWARDS FORFEITURE

  - Rewards forfeited if account is closed for cause (fraud, default).
  - Rewards NOT forfeited if account closed in good standing.
  - Per CFPB guidance (2024-07): Material devaluation of earned rewards without adequate notice may constitute an unfair or deceptive practice.

6.4 EXPIRATION

  - Venture Miles: Do not expire while account is open and in good standing.
  - Quicksilver cash back: Does not expire.
  - If account closed by Capital One: Remaining rewards typically forfeited within 30 days.
