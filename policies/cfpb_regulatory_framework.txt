CREDIT CARD POLICY KNOWLEDGE BASE
Document: CFPB Credit Card Consumer Rights & Regulatory Framework
Source: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) — 2024 Guidelines
Section: Federal Regulations for Credit Card Dispute Resolution

========================================================================
SECTION 1: REGULATION Z — TRUTH IN LENDING ACT (12 CFR PART 1026)
========================================================================

1.1 BILLING ERROR RESOLUTION (12 CFR § 1026.13)

A "billing error" includes:
  (a) A reflection on a periodic statement of an extension of credit that was not made to the consumer or to a person who had actual, implied, or apparent authority to use the consumer's credit card account.
  (b) A reflection on a periodic statement of an extension of credit that was not accepted by the consumer, or that was not used in conformity with the creditor's procedures.
  (c) A reflection on a periodic statement of an extension of credit for property or services not delivered to the consumer as agreed.
  (d) A computation error or similar clerical error.
  (e) Failure to reflect a payment or other credit to the consumer's account.
  (f) A reflection on a periodic statement of an extension of credit for which the consumer requests additional clarification.

CONSUMER RIGHTS UPON BILLING ERROR DISPUTE:
  - Consumer must notify creditor in WRITING within 60 days of the statement date.
  - Creditor must acknowledge written notice within 30 days.
  - Creditor must resolve dispute within TWO BILLING CYCLES (not to exceed 90 days).
  - During investigation, creditor CANNOT: attempt to collect disputed amount; restrict the account solely because of dispute; threaten to report adversely; or report the amount as delinquent.
  - If error confirmed: Creditor must correct the error and credit all related finance charges.
  - If no error found: Creditor must provide written explanation and make documents available.

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Failure to comply with billing error resolution requirements = creditor forfeits right to collect disputed amount AND related finance charges, up to $50.

1.2 UNAUTHORIZED USE LIABILITY (12 CFR § 1026.12(b))

  - Consumer's maximum liability for unauthorized use of a credit card is $50.
  - Consumer has ZERO liability if:
      (i) The card was not received by the consumer.
      (ii) It can be demonstrated that unauthorized use could not have occurred.
      (iii) The card issuer did not provide a means to identify the authorized user (e.g., signature, photo, PIN).
  - "Unauthorized use" means use by a person other than the cardholder who does not have actual, implied, or apparent authority.
  - Card issuers CANNOT hold consumers liable for unauthorized charges reported promptly.

1.3 REQUIRED DISCLOSURES (12 CFR § 1026.6, § 1026.7)

  - Annual Percentage Rate (APR) for purchases, balance transfers, cash advances.
  - Method of computing the balance on which finance charges are assessed.
  - Amount and conditions of any minimum finance charge.
  - Penalty APR triggers and conditions for rate reduction.
  - Grace period for paying without finance charge.
  - Minimum payment obligations and consequences of paying only minimum.

SECTION 2: FAIR CREDIT BILLING ACT (FCBA) — 15 U.S.C. §§ 1666–1666j
========================================================================

2.1 CONSUMER RIGHTS UNDER FCBA

  - The right to dispute billing errors within 60 days of statement.
  - Protection from adverse credit reporting during dispute period.
  - Right to withhold payment on disputed amounts during investigation.
  - Right to a written explanation if creditor determines no error.

2.2 MERCHANTS AND CREDITOR OBLIGATIONS

  If consumer is dissatisfied with goods or services purchased on credit card:
  - Consumer may refuse to pay remaining amount IF:
      (a) Consumer made good faith effort to resolve problem with merchant.
      (b) Original sale was for more than $50.
      (c) Sale occurred in consumer's home state or within 100 miles of billing address (this geographic restriction does NOT apply if merchant is same as card issuer).
  - Consumer must notify creditor of dispute.

2.3 PROHIBITED ACTS BY CREDITORS

  Creditors may NOT:
  - Report disputed amounts to credit bureaus as delinquent during investigation.
  - Accelerate payment on disputed amounts.
  - Close account solely because consumer disputed a charge.
  - Threaten or pursue collection activities on disputed amounts during investigation period.
  - Assess late fees on disputed amount during investigation period.

SECTION 3: EQUAL CREDIT OPPORTUNITY ACT (ECOA) — 15 U.S.C. § 1691
========================================================================

3.1 PROHIBITED DISCRIMINATION

  Creditors may NOT discriminate on basis of:
  - Race, color, religion, national origin
  - Sex or marital status
  - Age (if consumer is of legal age to contract)
  - Because consumer receives public assistance
  - Consumer's exercise of rights under Consumer Credit Protection Act

3.2 ADVERSE ACTION REQUIREMENTS

  If creditor takes adverse action (denies credit, reduces limit, closes account):
  - Must provide written notice of adverse action.
  - Must state specific reasons for adverse action OR inform consumer of right to request reasons.
  - Must provide reasons within 30 days if consumer requests them.
  - "Adverse action" includes: denial, termination, unfavorable change in terms (unless applied to all similarly situated consumers).

3.3 DISPARATE IMPACT STANDARD

  Even neutral policies may violate ECOA if they have a disproportionate adverse impact on a protected class AND the creditor cannot justify the policy as necessary for a legitimate business purpose. Lenders must assess whether criteria have disparate impact and whether alternative criteria with less disparate impact exist.

SECTION 4: CREDIT CARD ACCOUNTABILITY RESPONSIBILITY AND DISCLOSURE ACT (CARD ACT) — 2009
========================================================================

4.1 RATE INCREASE RESTRICTIONS

  - Creditors CANNOT raise APR on existing balances (retroactive rate increases prohibited).
  - Exception: After 60 days of delinquency, but rate must be restored if consumer makes 6 consecutive minimum payments on time.
  - Must give 45 days advance notice before any rate increase on new transactions.
  - Consumer has right to opt out (close account) when rate is raised; must be allowed to pay off existing balance at old rate.

4.2 FEE RESTRICTIONS

  - Late fees capped at $32 (adjusted for inflation; CFPB proposed $8 in 2024 for large issuers).
  - Over-limit fees: Consumer must affirmatively opt in to over-limit coverage. Otherwise, transaction over limit must be declined.
  - No inactivity fees (fees for not using account).
  - Annual fees and other fees must be reasonable and proportional.

4.3 STATEMENT AND NOTICE REQUIREMENTS

  - Statements must be mailed/delivered at least 21 days before payment due date.
  - Must show consequences of paying only minimum payment (time to pay off and interest cost).
  - Must include "minimum payment warning."
  - Toll-free number for credit counseling agencies required on statements.

4.4 PROTECTIONS FOR YOUNG CONSUMERS

  - Credit cards cannot be issued to applicants under 21 without: (a) independent income to repay, or (b) cosigner who is 21+.
  - No prescreened solicitations to consumers under 21 unless consumer opts in.

SECTION 5: CFPB EXAMINATION PRIORITIES — CREDIT CARD ACCOUNTS (2024)
========================================================================

5.1 UNFAIR, DECEPTIVE, OR ABUSIVE ACTS OR PRACTICES (UDAAP)

  CFPB examiners focus on whether card issuers:
  - Misrepresent terms, benefits, or limitations of credit card products.
  - Fail to honor rewards, cashback, or other promotional promises.
  - Devalue earned rewards without adequate notice.
  - Use confusing or misleading disclosures in marketing.
  - Apply fees not disclosed at account opening.

5.2 COMPLAINT RESOLUTION STANDARDS

  CFPB examines whether issuers:
  - Have adequate complaint intake processes.
  - Investigate complaints promptly and thoroughly.
  - Communicate responses clearly and in plain language.
  - Track complaint patterns to identify systemic issues.
  - Provide monetary relief when errors are confirmed.

5.3 FAIR LENDING REVIEWS

  Examiners analyze:
  - Whether underwriting criteria correlates with protected class status.
  - Whether similarly situated consumers receive inconsistent treatment.
  - Pricing disparities correlated with protected characteristics.
  - Whether exceptions disproportionately benefit certain groups.
