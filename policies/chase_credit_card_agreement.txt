CREDIT CARD POLICY KNOWLEDGE BASE
Document: Chase Credit Card Cardholder Agreement — Key Provisions
Source: JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A. — Standard Credit Card Agreement (2024)
Applicable Cards: Chase Sapphire, Chase Freedom, Chase Ink Business, Chase Amazon

========================================================================
SECTION 1: BILLING DISPUTE RIGHTS (YOUR BILLING RIGHTS NOTICE)
========================================================================

1.1 WHAT TO DO IF YOU FIND A MISTAKE ON YOUR STATEMENT

If you think there is an error on your statement, write to us at:
  Chase Card Services, PO Box 15299, Wilmington, DE 19850-5299

In your letter, provide:
  - Your name and account number
  - The dollar amount of the suspected error
  - A description of the suspected error and explanation of why you believe it is an error

You must contact us:
  - Within 60 DAYS after the error appeared on your statement.
  - At least 3 business days before an automated payment is scheduled.

1.2 WHAT HAPPENS DURING THE DISPUTE PERIOD

While we investigate:
  - We cannot try to collect the disputed amount.
  - We cannot report the disputed amount as delinquent.
  - We cannot restrict or close your account just because of a dispute.
  - The disputed amount may remain on your statement. We will not charge late fees on it.

We must resolve the dispute within two billing cycles (not more than 90 days).

If we determine no error:
  - We will send you a written explanation.
  - You may request documentation we used to make our decision.
  - You will owe the disputed amount plus any applicable finance charges accrued.

If we determine an error occurred:
  - We will correct your account.
  - We will credit all related finance charges.

1.3 UNAUTHORIZED CHARGES

Your liability for unauthorized use of your credit card:
  - MAXIMUM LIABILITY: $50 for unauthorized charges made before you notify us.
  - ZERO LIABILITY for unauthorized charges made after you notify us of loss or theft.
  - Chase's Zero Liability Policy: Chase extends ZERO liability for unauthorized use in most cases, provided you were not grossly negligent or fraudulent.

How to report: Call the number on the back of your card immediately if your card is lost, stolen, or used without authorization.

SECTION 2: DEFAULT, PENALTY RATES, AND FEES
========================================================================

2.1 EVENTS OF DEFAULT

Your account may be considered in default if you:
  - Fail to make a minimum payment by the due date.
  - Exceed your credit limit.
  - Make a payment that is later dishonored (returned/bounced).
  - Provide false information on your application.
  - File for bankruptcy protection.
  - Fail to follow any other terms of this Agreement.

2.2 PENALTY APR (Applicable to new transactions after default)

  - Penalty APR: Up to 29.99% variable
  - When it applies: If you make a late payment.
  - How long it applies: Indefinitely, but we will review your account every 6 months. If you made minimum payments on time for 6 consecutive months, we will reinstate your standard APR.

2.3 FEES (Standard Schedule)

  Late Payment Fee:
    - Up to $40 for accounts where late fee was charged in any of prior 6 billing cycles.
    - $30 for first occurrence.

  Returned Payment Fee:
    - Up to $40.

  Cash Advance Fee:
    - Either $10 or 5% of each cash advance, whichever is greater.

  Balance Transfer Fee:
    - Either $5 or 5% of each balance transfer, whichever is greater.
    - Promotional transfer rates may have different fees — see offer details.

  Foreign Transaction Fee:
    - 3% of each transaction in U.S. dollars (waived on Sapphire and Ink Preferred cards).

  Over-Limit: Chase does NOT permit transactions over your credit limit in most cases (transactions are declined). There is no over-limit fee.

========================================================================
SECTION 3: INTEREST RATES AND CALCULATIONS
========================================================================

3.1 ANNUAL PERCENTAGE RATES (Variable Rates — tied to Prime Rate)

  - Purchase APR: Prime Rate + 15.74% to Prime Rate + 24.74% (based on creditworthiness)
  - Balance Transfer APR: Same as Purchase APR in most cases.
  - Cash Advance APR: Prime Rate + 21.74% to Prime Rate + 26.74%
  - Penalty APR: Up to 29.99%

Prime Rate used: Highest U.S. Prime Rate published in The Wall Street Journal on the last business day of each calendar month.

3.2 GRACE PERIOD

  - If you pay your full balance by the due date each month, you will NOT be charged interest on new purchases.
  - Grace period: At least 21 days from close of billing cycle to due date.
  - No grace period applies to cash advances or balance transfers (interest accrues from transaction date).

3.3 MINIMUM PAYMENT

  - Greater of: $35 OR 1% of the new balance plus fees, interest, and any past-due amounts.
  - OR full balance if less than $35.
  - Paying only the minimum substantially increases total interest paid and time to pay off balance.

========================================================================
SECTION 4: ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT RIGHTS
========================================================================

4.1 CHASE'S RIGHT TO CHANGE TERMS

  - Chase may change terms (including rates, fees) with 45 days advance notice.
  - For rate increases on new transactions: Consumer has 45 days to opt out (close account and pay off at current terms).
  - For rate increases on existing balances: PROHIBITED under CARD Act, except after 60 days of delinquency.

4.2 ACCOUNT CLOSURE

  Either party may close the account at any time:
  - Consumer: Call or write to close. Closing does not eliminate existing balance obligations.
  - Chase: May close account for violation of Agreement terms. Will not close solely because of dispute.
  - After closure: Existing balance remains subject to Agreement terms.

4.3 CREDIT LIMIT CHANGES

  - Chase may increase or decrease credit limit at any time.
  - Chase will notify consumer of decreases that affect transactions.
  - If a transaction causes account to exceed credit limit (in rare cases): Consumer responsible for full amount.

========================================================================
SECTION 5: REWARDS PROGRAM TERMS
========================================================================

5.1 POINTS EXPIRATION AND FORFEITURE

  - Points do not expire as long as account is open and in good standing.
  - If account is CLOSED (by consumer or Chase): Points forfeited within 30 days (Sapphire: may be transferable).
  - Points may be forfeited if account is delinquent, in default, or involved in fraud.

5.2 REWARDS DISPUTES

  - If points are missing or incorrectly applied: Contact Chase within 6 months of the statement period.
  - Per CFPB Circular 2024-07: Materially devaluing rewards without adequate notice may be an unfair or deceptive practice.

5.3 ANNUAL FEE REFUND POLICY

  - If you close a Sapphire account within 30 days of the annual fee posting, Chase will refund the annual fee.

========================================================================
SECTION 6: ARBITRATION AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
========================================================================

6.1 ARBITRATION CLAUSE

  - Chase's agreements include an arbitration clause for disputes between consumer and Chase.
  - Consumer may OPT OUT of arbitration within 60 days of account opening by writing to Chase.
  - Arbitration prohibits class action lawsuits — each dispute must be resolved individually.
  - Small claims court option: Either party may bring claims in small claims court.

6.2 APPLICABLE LAW

  - Agreement governed by laws of Delaware and applicable Federal law.
  - Regulation Z, FCBA, and ECOA apply regardless of arbitration clause.
